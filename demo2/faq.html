Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Preston Consults Data Repository

General Questions

What is the Preston Consults Data Repository?

The Preston Consults Data Repository is a comprehensive platform providing socio-economic data and development indicators for African countries. It offers interactive tools to explore, analyze, and export data across multiple sectors including economic, social, health, education, technology, and trade indicators.

Which countries are covered?

Currently, our repository covers four major African economies: Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, and Egypt. We are continuously expanding our coverage to include additional countries across the continent.

How often is the data updated?

Data is updated on a quarterly basis, with annual reviews to ensure accuracy and completeness. Critical indicators may be updated more frequently based on data availability from our partner organizations.

Data Access & Usage

Is the data free to access?

Yes, the Preston Consults Data Repository is completely free to access. We believe in democratizing access to quality data for research, policy-making, and strategic decision-making.

Can I download the data?

Absolutely! You can export your filtered datasets as CSV files directly from both the All Data Explorer and Trade Data Explorer. Simply apply your filters, click "Show Results," and then click the "Export Data" button.

How do I cite the data in my research?

Please cite as: Preston Consults Ltd Research and Data Analysis Team (2025). Preston Consults Data Repository. [Indicator Name]. Retrieved from https://prestonconsults.github.io/data-explorer/

Additionally, please cite the original data source (e.g., World Bank, IMF) as indicated in the "Source" column of each indicator.

Using the Explorers

What's the difference between All Data Explorer and Trade Data Explorer?

All Data Explorer: Contains comprehensive multi-sector data covering economic indicators (GDP, inflation, unemployment), social development, health, education, technology, and environmental metrics.

Trade Data Explorer: Specialized platform focusing exclusively on trade-related indicators including exports, imports, trade balance, tariffs, regional trade agreements, and competitiveness indices.

What are Annual, Quarterly, and Monthly timeframes?

Annual: Data aggregated by calendar year (e.g., 2018, 2019, 2020)

Quarterly: Data broken down by quarters (e.g., 2018Q1, 2018Q2, 2018Q3, 2018Q4)

Monthly: Most granular data available by month (e.g., 2018-01, 2018-02)

Choose the timeframe that best suits your analysis needs. When you select a year in the filter, the system automatically expands it to show the relevant quarters or months.

How do I use the filters?
  1. Select your desired Timeframe (Annual, Quarterly, or Monthly)
  2. Choose Indicators you want to analyze (use the search box to find specific ones)
  3. Select Countries you're interested in
  4. Choose Years for your time period
  5. Click "Show Results" to display your filtered data
  6. Switch between Table View and Chart View as needed
What does "..." mean in the data table?

The "..." symbol indicates missing or unavailable data for that specific indicator, country, and time period. This can occur when data hasn't been reported by the source organization or hasn't been collected for that period.

Data Sources & Reliability

Where does the data come from?

Our data is sourced from leading international organizations including:

  • World Bank
  • International Monetary Fund (IMF)
  • International Labour Organization (ILO)
  • United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD)
  • World Trade Organization (WTO)
  • International Telecommunication Union (ITU)
  • World Health Organization (WHO)
  • UNESCO
  • African Development Bank
  • UN Comtrade
How reliable is the data?

All data comes from internationally recognized organizations that follow rigorous collection and validation methodologies. Each indicator includes source attribution so you can verify the original data. However, please note that data quality can vary by country and indicator based on local statistical capacity.
